UniBites

A research-driven campus food recovery platform designed to reduce food waste by connecting students with surplus meals from events, dining, and local businesses.

Research Presentation (PDF) Prototype (Independently Developed)

Objective

UniBites aimed to address food waste at the university level by exploring whether a campus-focused model similar to Too Good To Go could be viable for students, dining services, and local businesses. The project focused on identifying real gaps through primary and secondary research, then translating those insights into a practical, student-centered solution.

Development Process

💡 Planning & Ideation

Our team followed the design thinking process throughout the semester, beginning with extensive research into food waste on campus. We conducted interviews with local restaurants, campus organizations, and food pantries to understand operational constraints, willingness to participate, and potential barriers. Research findings suggested strong interest in a platform that could redistribute surplus food efficiently while remaining accessible to students.

💡 Implementation

Based on research insights, I independently designed a comprehensive interactive prototype that translated our findings into a cohesive user experience. The prototype included multiple discovery and ordering pathways, such as an interactive map, list-based browsing, and a social-style feed for sharing food availability. The full end-to-end flow—from discovery to checkout and order confirmation—was designed and built solely by me.

💡 Challenges & Solutions

Design Challenge: Designing a system flexible enough to support multiple food sources (campus events, restaurants, and informal food sharing) while keeping the experience simple for students.

The Solution: I introduced complementary browsing patterns such as map, list, and feed-based discovery, enabling flexible user engagement while preserving consistency across the experience.

Project Impact

UniBites demonstrates how research-driven design can translate complex social and logistical challenges into an intuitive digital experience. The project highlights my ability to synthesize qualitative research, design complete user flows independently, and create scalable concepts grounded in real stakeholder needs.

  • Conducted primary and secondary research with local businesses, food pantries, and campus stakeholders.
  • Designed a full end-to-end prototype independently, from discovery to order confirmation.
  • Translated research insights into multiple interaction models (map, list, and feed-based browsing).
  • Applied design thinking methodology to a real-world sustainability problem.

Development Reflection

Looking back, UniBites evolved from a broad exploration of campus food waste into a fully realized platform concept grounded in real research. Designing the complete prototype independently challenged me to think critically about scalability, accessibility, and user motivation. The project strengthened my confidence in owning the full UX process, from research synthesis to high-fidelity interaction design while working within a collaborative, research-driven team environment.
