Serve IT: Teach IT

A community-focused learning project introducing elementary-aged students to foundational technology concepts through interactive lessons.

Game Development Lesson Slides TeachIT Team Poster (PDF)

Objective

The goal of this project was to introduce elementary-aged students at one of the local Bloomington Boys & Girls Club to foundational technology concepts in a fun, interactive way. As part of the Laurie Burns McRobbie Serve IT Clinic, our Teach IT team partnered with the local community to design and deliver weekly lesson plans focused on digital literacy, problem-solving, and early exposure to computer science topics. The project emphasized accessibility, engagement, and creating a supportive learning environment for students with little to no prior experience in technology.

Implementation

Throughout the semester, my team and I visited local Boys & Girls Club locations weekly to deliver interactive technology-focused lessons. Each team member was responsible for designing and presenting their own lesson plan tailored to an elementary-aged audience. My lesson focused on introducing basic game development concepts, including the ideas of development and programming, using age-appropriate vocabulary and examples.

To reinforce these concepts, students watched a short, child-friendly video explaining game development, followed by a hands-on activity using Rodocodo, a free drag-and-drop coding game. In the activity, students created sequences of movement commands and tested their solutions by running their code to guide a character to a goal. This approach allowed students to practice logical thinking and problem-solving while engaging with core programming concepts in an accessible, playful format.

Results

This project strengthened my ability to communicate technical ideas to non-technical audiences and reinforced the importance of inclusive, user-centered design especially when working with young learners. Through lesson planning and classroom facilitation, I developed skills in adaptability, teamwork, and client-facing communication while gaining hands-on experience in community-engaged technology education.

  • Designed and delivered engaging, age-appropriate lesson plans on game development and digital literacy
  • Introduced foundational programming concepts using visual, drag-and-drop tools
  • Encouraged problem-solving, teamwork, and curiosity through hands-on activities
  • Gained experience working with community partners in a real-world, client-focused setting
