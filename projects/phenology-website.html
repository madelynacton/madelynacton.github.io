Indiana Phenology Website

An interactive educational website engaging K-12 students with phenology and citizen science.

Live Site

Objective

The goal of the project was to create an educational and interactive website for K-12 students to explore phenology data through dashboards, maps, and learning activities, while providing a scalable design system for future semesters.

Development Process

💡 Planning & Ideation

My team and I conducted research on educational and interactive websites for K-12 audiences, analyzing competitors like the National Phenology Network and iNaturalist. Wireframes and prototypes were polished in Figma before beginning development in WordPress.

💡 Implementation

Using Figma for design and WordPress for development, the project focused on building pages like the Home Page and Tulip Tree Historical View. Agile practices, including sprint planning and Kanban boards, were applied to manage workflow and coordinate the team.

💡 Challenges & Solutions

Technical Challenge: Translating complex phenology data into interactive, child-friendly visualizations.

The Solution: Iteratively refined Figma prototypes, prioritized accessibility, and collaborated closely with the client to ensure clarity and usability.

Project Impact

The website successfully created an engaging platform for K-12 students to explore phenology, integrating interactive dashboards and visualizations. Agile workflow and collaborative design practices ensured a scalable and user-centered outcome.

  • Interactive dashboards for phenology tracking implemented in Figma and WordPress.
  • Enhanced leadership, client communication, and Agile project management skills.
  • Improved ability to balance user-friendly design with complex scientific data.

Development Reflection

I had the privilege of experiencing working with the Indiana Phenology web team for two semesters. The project has greatly evolved from early research and wireframes to a functional, interactive website with a consistent design system and accessible interface, ready for future student teams to expand upon.
