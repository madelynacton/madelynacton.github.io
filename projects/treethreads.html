TreeThreads

A mid-fidelity UX prototype for a sustainable clothing marketplace that incentivizes eco-friendly buying and selling behaviors.

Prototype

Objective

The goal of TreeThreads was to explore how human-centered design principles can influence user behavior and promote sustainability. As part of an introductory Human Computer Interaction and Design course, my team was tasked with identifying a real-world problem and designing a solution that prioritizes user needs, motivations, and accessibility.

My group focused on clothing consumerism and sustainability, aiming to reduce textile waste by encouraging secondhand buying and selling while rewarding users for environmentally responsible actions.

Implementation

TreeThreads was designed as a mid-fidelity prototype that emphasizes usability, clarity, and behavior-driven design. Early in the process, we applied foundational HCI concepts—such as affordances, feedback, and mental models—to ensure the interface felt intuitive to first-time users. Classic examples like the “doorknob problem” helped guide our design decisions by reminding us that good design should communicate function without explanation.

We structured the platform similarly to familiar resale apps, allowing users to browse, list, and purchase clothing with minimal friction. To encourage sustainable behavior, we introduced a rewards system where users earn “leaves” for actions such as using eco-friendly packaging or choosing secondhand items. These incentives were intentionally integrated into the user flow to reinforce positive habits without overwhelming the experience.

The final prototype was created collaboratively, with a strong focus on user-centered decision making, visual hierarchy, and consistency across screens.

Results

The final TreeThreads prototype successfully demonstrates how thoughtful interface design can encourage sustainable consumer behavior. By combining familiar marketplace patterns with subtle incentive mechanisms, the platform lowers the barrier to secondhand shopping while promoting environmentally conscious choices.

This project strengthened my understanding of how design decisions directly affect user behavior and reinforced the importance of designing with empathy, clarity, and purpose.

  • Designed a mid-fidelity prototype grounded in core HCID principles
  • Applied user-centered design concepts such as affordances, feedback, and mental models
  • Explored how incentives can shape user behavior in digital products
  • Collaborated in a team setting to ideate, iterate, and refine a shared design vision
  • Gained foundational experience in prototyping and UX design thinking
